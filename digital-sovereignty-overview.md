  ## What is Digital Sovereignty?
  Digital sovereignty encompasses a set of requirements that government bodies, enterprises, and regulated organizations place on their digital infrastructure to ensure data, services, and personnel remain within defined geographic, legal, or security boundaries.

  Regulations driving these requirements include:

  
    
      
        ### Americas
        
          - United States — FedRAMP, HIPAA, Gramm-Leach-Bliley, CCPA
          - Canada — Critical Cyber Systems Protection Act, PIPEDA, Protected C
        
        ### Europe
        
          - European Union — GDPR
          - Italy — ACN (Agenzia per la Cybersicurezza Nazionale)
          - Netherlands — BIO (Baseline Informatiebeveiliging Overheid)
          - Spain — ENS (Esquema Nacional de Seguridad)
        
      
    
    
      
        ### Asia Pacific
        
          - Australia — Privacy Act 1988, IRAP, Critical Infrastructure Act 2021
          - China — Cybersecurity Law, MLPS 2.0, PIPL
          - India — IT Act Section 70, DPDP Act 2023
          - Japan — APPI 2022, ISMAP
          - Singapore — PDPA 2012
        
        ### Middle East
        
          - Saudi Arabia — Personal Data Protection Law, Cybersecurity Law
          - United Arab Emirates — Personal Data Protection Law

        AEM Digital Sovereignty offerings in supported countries include separate and independent infrastructure, personnel, and services to enable governments and corporations to meet each country's stringent requirements. Governance and auditing tools deliver the necessary capabilities to review and report on regulated data and application behavior. Log and telemetry aggregation facilitates the sharing of logs and telemetry with customer logging systems or SIEM apparatus.

      
    
    
      
        ### Identity and Access Management
        IAM Services work seamlessly with existing databases via OAuth 2.0, OpenID Connect, and SAML for a single source of truth on identity and access data.         Cloud-based containers help to isolate data during processing. Container images are scanned for vulnerabilities, malware, and compliance violations during development, deployment, and at runtime. Adobe's Digital Sovereignty detection and response capabilities identify and link security data from multiple layers to augment the security team's ability to recognize and respond to complex attacks faster.

  ## Implementation Details
  
    - Customers can route end-user traffic via their own network (CDN and Managed Gateway) to the publish tier over a completely private network link.
    - Authoring and intranet users can access the service via a private network link from multiple corporate network locations, mapped to an IP address on the customer network.
    - Customers can purchase Managed CDN/WAF or bring their own CDN/WAF. Adobe provides an additional WAF layer at the load balancer tier.
    - Log and telemetry aggregation facilitates the sharing of logs and telemetry with customer logging systems or SIEM apparatus. Data feeds can be filtered, transformed, redacted, and routed to multiple destinations.
    - SSH access to AEM topologies is facilitated by an integrated bastion/jump host solution which can be integrated with customer IDP for customer-managed access, confined to the private network via VPC endpoint services.
    - Customer Threat Intelligence feeds can be integrated into AMS security tooling to allow real-time threat sharing, so AEM systems can be proactively protected from customer-detected threats without delay.
    - Setup and configuration of Digital Sovereignty will be managed in-region by an assigned Customer Success Engineer (CSE).
