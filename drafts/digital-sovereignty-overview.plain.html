What is Digital Sovereignty?

Digital sovereignty encompasses a set of requirements that government bodies, enterprises, and regulated organizations place on their digital infrastructure to ensure data, services, and personnel remain within defined geographic, legal, or security boundaries.

Regulations driving these requirements include:

Americas

  • United States — FedRAMP, HIPAA, Gramm-Leach-Bliley, CCPA
  • Canada — Critical Cyber Systems Protection Act, PIPEDA, Protected C

Europe

  • European Union — GDPR
  • Italy — ACN (Agenzia per la Cybersicurezza Nazionale)
  • Netherlands — BIO (Baseline Informatiebeveiliging Overheid)
  • Spain — ENS (Esquema Nacional de Seguridad)

Asia Pacific

  • Australia — Privacy Act 1988, IRAP, Critical Infrastructure Act 2021
  • China — Cybersecurity Law, MLPS 2.0, PIPL
  • India — IT Act Section 70, DPDP Act 2023
  • Japan — APPI 2022, ISMAP
  • Singapore — PDPA 2012

Middle East

  • Saudi Arabia — Personal Data Protection Law, Cybersecurity Law
  • United Arab Emirates — Personal Data Protection Law

About Digital Sovereignty for AEM Managed Services

Rapid Deployment

Deploy an AEM Digital Sovereignty service into a new jurisdiction in a week using automated tooling, powered by single-tenant command and control — instead of months as an on-premises solution or do-it-yourself set of unintegrated cloud components.

Extensive Cloud Regions

AEM sovereignty can be deployed to dozens of available Amazon AWS, Microsoft Azure, and GCP sovereign regions. AEM sovereignty is also capable of being deployed to non-sovereign public cloud regions with added encryption and controls. All sovereignty customers benefit from a single-tenant design with network firewalls, secure encryption, and monitoring for strong data isolation.

Regulatory Compliance, Governance, and Auditing

AEM Digital Sovereignty offerings in supported countries include separate and independent infrastructure, personnel, and services to enable governments and corporations to meet each country's stringent requirements. Governance and auditing tools deliver the necessary capabilities to review and report on regulated data and application behavior. Log and telemetry aggregation facilitates the sharing of logs and telemetry with customer logging systems or SIEM apparatus.

Identity and Access Management

IAM Services work seamlessly with existing databases via OAuth 2.0, OpenID Connect, and SAML for a single source of truth on identity and access data. Maximize CIAM security and minimize user frustration with support for SSO, password-less logins, multi-factor authentication, and linked social media accounts.

Enhanced Container Security

Cloud-based containers help to isolate data during processing. Container images are scanned for vulnerabilities, malware, and compliance violations during development, deployment, and at runtime. Containers are monitored continuously while running to check for new vulnerabilities and conformance to current data security policies.

Extended Detection and Response

Modern cyberthreat defense solutions use multiple layers to detect and neutralize threats across the full attack surface. Adobe's Digital Sovereignty detection and response capabilities identify and link security data from multiple layers to augment the security team's ability to recognize and respond to complex attacks faster.

Threat Intelligence

Adobe receives real-time threat intelligence from its network of cybersecurity partners and translates that into automated vulnerability remediation against malware and other security threats. Preventive techniques leverage a worldwide reputation database of suspicious and malicious domains, URLs, and IP addresses to facilitate faster detection of infiltration, exfiltration, and command and control traffic.

Cleared Personnel

Adobe staff with relevant security clearances (Secret, TS-SCI) and experience deploying AEM on sovereign architectures are available for specialized scenarios requiring personnel with more stringent clearances.

Personnel Localization and Identity

Adobe customers can utilize the expertise of named and known talent who are citizens of or physically available within the boundaries of the country or region — complying with sovereignty regulations on physical environment access, operations, and management.

High Security Classifications

Exceed stringent requirements such as U.S. FedRAMP+ and Canada's Protected C classification, supporting compliance with the most demanding national security standards — enabling governments, defense agencies, and regulated industries to securely operate mission-critical workloads.

High-Side Deployment

AEM can be deployed into high-side environments to support the most sensitive government and defense workloads. These deployments can be fully air-gapped and operate within customer-controlled networks, ensuring no exposure to the public internet. Controlled access is enforced through cleared personnel, strict identity management, and role-based permissions.

Compliance Customization

Data sovereignty regulations are often dynamic and updated in response to social or technological changes. Adobe works with customers to make the Digital Sovereignty offering compliant with new or local regulations not already supported — including uncommon and emerging data regulations and standards.

Implementation Details

  • Customers can route end-user traffic via their own network (CDN and Managed Gateway) to the publish tier over a completely private network link.
  • Authoring and intranet users can access the service via a private network link from multiple corporate network locations, mapped to an IP address on the customer network.
  • Customers can purchase Managed CDN/WAF or bring their own CDN/WAF. Adobe provides an additional WAF layer at the load balancer tier.
  • Log and telemetry aggregation facilitates the sharing of logs and telemetry with customer logging systems or SIEM apparatus. Data feeds can be filtered, transformed, redacted, and routed to multiple destinations.
  • SSH access to AEM topologies is facilitated by an integrated bastion/jump host solution which can be integrated with customer IDP for customer-managed access, confined to the private network via VPC endpoint services.
  • Customer Threat Intelligence feeds can be integrated into AMS security tooling to allow real-time threat sharing, so AEM systems can be proactively protected from customer-detected threats without delay.
  • Setup and configuration of Digital Sovereignty will be managed in-region by an assigned Customer Success Engineer (CSE).
